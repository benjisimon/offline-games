# Bear Heist — Twists

Drop these mid-heist when things need to get worse. Roll or pick.

Roll 2d6 to pick: first die = section (1–5, reroll 6), second die = scenario within section (1–5, reroll 6).

---

## Section 1: The Setup Is Already Wrong

1. One bear ate the plan. The actual paper it was written on. You have to
   improvise.

2. The honey has already been stolen — by the bees. They want it back. They
   are organized.

3. The vault opened on schedule — but it's empty. Someone got here first. The
   honey trail leads deeper into the convention center.

4. There's a second heist happening simultaneously. Different bears. They think
   you're competition. You might need to work together.

5. The getaway driver fell asleep in the car. The parking garage closes in
   ten minutes.

---

## Section 2: The Organizer Is a Problem

1. The organizer is your old zookeeper. She definitely recognizes you.

2. The organizer is also a bear. She's been running this scam for years. She
   wants a cut.

3. The vault is voice-activated. It only opens for the organizer's voice. She
   is currently giving a keynote to 300 people.

4. The security system was designed by the honey manufacturer specifically to
   stop bears. They've clearly dealt with this before.

5. The convention is also a police sting operation. Not the bee kind.

---

## Section 3: Logistics Gone Wrong

1. The honey is real, the vault is accessible — but one bear has accidentally
   become the convention's guest of honor.

2. HoneyCon is on a cruise ship. The honey vault is below the waterline and
   floods at high tide.

3. The honey is at the top of a twelve-story building. The elevator requires a
   keycard. The stairs are blocked for a charity run.

4. The keynote is ending thirty minutes early. You have half the time you
   planned for.

5. One bear's disguise is perfect. The others are not. You all have to go
   through the front door at the same time.

---

## Section 4: Unexpected Complications

1. A child at the convention keeps following your most suspicious bear and
   asking questions.

2. A documentarian is filming everything at the convention. She is very
   thorough.

3. A "Find the Bear" kids' game is running throughout the convention. Two
   hundred children are actively looking for bears. There are prizes.

4. The security team is also bears — rival bears who got here first.

5. One bear has developed a conscience. Mid-heist.

---

## Section 5: The Honey Itself Is the Problem

1. The honey is real but it's inside an active beehive. With bees.

2. The prize honey is sentient. It has opinions about being stolen.

3. A bee hospitality booth is giving out free samples near the vault entrance.
   This is a problem for at least two of the bears.

4. Security is neutralized, the honey is in your paws — and then the actual
   bees who made it show up. All of them.

5. The honey is real, the plan worked, everyone is almost out — and then the
   honey starts glowing.
