# Lasers & Feelings — Twists

Drop these mid-session when the action needs a jolt. Roll or pick.

Roll 2d6 to pick: first die = section (1–5, reroll 6), second die = scenario within section (1–5, reroll 6).

---

## Section 1: The Mission Changes

1. The thing you were sent to destroy turns out to be alive.

2. The person you were sent to rescue doesn't want to be rescued.

3. The Consortium issued new orders while you were out of range. They
   contradict everything you've done so far.

4. The threat you've been chasing was a decoy. The real one is behind you.

5. The mission was a test. Someone has been watching the whole time.

---

## Section 2: Someone Can't Be Trusted

1. One crew member receives a private transmission and won't say who it was
   from.

2. The ally who's been helping you is also working for the threat.

3. Captain Darcy briefly regains consciousness and says one sentence before
   going under again. It's about one of the crew members.

4. The Consortium contact who gave you this mission has gone dark.

5. Something Else — the entity possessing Captain Darcy — starts talking
   through him. It claims it's trying to help.

---

## Section 3: The Ship Is the Problem

1. The Raptor's warp drive goes offline at the worst possible moment.

2. An unknown ship has been shadowing the Raptor for the last six hours. It
   just accelerated.

3. The ship's logs show it visited a location no one remembers going to.

4. One console explodes on the bridge. Whatever it controlled is now gone.

5. The Raptor receives a hail from itself — from thirty minutes in the future.

---

## Section 4: The Threat Escalates

1. Reinforcements arrive for the enemy. More than expected. Much more.

2. The threat offers a deal. It's a good deal. Taking it would end the mission.

3. Something the crew did earlier in the session has made things significantly
   worse. The threat knows what you tried.

4. The enemy has a hostage. It's someone specific — roll a d6 and pick a crew
   member's backstory detail.

5. The threat isn't what it appeared to be. Destroying it would cause the very
   thing you're trying to prevent.

---

## Section 5: Strange & Weird

1. A crew member rolls LASER FEELINGS and gets an answer that doesn't make
   sense yet — the GM banks it for later.

2. Gravity on part of the ship reverses. No explanation. It stops after ten
   minutes.

3. An alien artifact activates in response to something the crew just did.

4. A dead crew member — from a previous mission, campaign, or backstory —
   sends a message.

5. The stars outside the viewscreen are wrong. The Raptor is not where the
   nav computer thinks it is.
