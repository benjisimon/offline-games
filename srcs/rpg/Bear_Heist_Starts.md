# Bear Heist — Opening Scenarios

Roll 2d6 to pick a scenario: first die = section (1–5, reroll 6), second die
= scenario within section (1–5, reroll 6).

---

## Section 1: You're Already Inside

1. The bears are in position, disguises on, thirty seconds before the plan
   begins — and one bear realizes they have the wrong keycard.

2. The crew has successfully infiltrated the convention as vendors. Their booth
   is directly across from the honey vault. The organizer just stopped to chat.

3. The bears are in the service corridor behind the main hall. The plan was to
   wait for the shift change. The shift change happened an hour early.

4. Everyone is in place. The signal to go is a sneeze from the bear nearest the
   vault. Someone already sneezed by accident.

5. The bears are dressed as convention staff and have full access — but their
   fake badges say they work in a department that doesn't exist.

---

## Section 2: Arriving at the Scene

1. The bears pull up to HoneyCon in a catering van. The real caterers are
   parked right next to them.

2. The convention is busier than expected — three times the usual attendance.
   The plan assumed a crowd of 200. There are 600.

3. The crew arrives to find the venue has changed. The honey is in the new
   location. No one updated the map.

4. HoneyCon is on the third floor. The elevator is out. The stairs require a
   convention badge to access. The bears have the wrong badges.

5. The bears arrive in disguise to find the convention has a dress code. Every
   legitimate attendee is wearing a lanyard the crew doesn't have.

---

## Section 3: The Brief

1. The job is simple: the honey is in a locked display case in the main hall,
   surrounded by 300 people. The case has no lock — it's bolted to the floor.

2. The honey is real, the vault is accessible, and the organizer leaves at
   noon for lunch every day. It is currently 11:58.

3. The prize is Abraham Lincoln's beehive, thought to be haunted by his ghost.
   It is in a glass case. The glass case is alarmed. The ghost is apparently
   also alarmed.

4. The honey is being auctioned at 3pm. The crew has until then to steal it
   before it changes hands to someone much harder to steal from.

5. The target is a briefcase of pure manuka extract worth $5 million. It is
   handcuffed to the organizer's wrist.

---

## Section 4: Something Is Already Off

1. One bear got a text twenty minutes ago: *"I know what you're planning."*
   No sender. No follow-up.

2. The crew's inside contact — a honey tasting judge — didn't show up. Her
   phone goes to voicemail. The plan depended on her.

3. There's a news crew filming a documentary about HoneyCon security. They
   are very enthusiastic.

4. The organizer has hired extra security today. Twice as many as last year.
   No announcement. No explanation.

5. The plan called for a distraction at 1pm. Someone already set off the
   distraction. At 11am. It worked. Now everyone is on high alert.

---

## Section 5: Wild Locations

1. HoneyCon is being held on a lake — on a floating platform. The getaway
   car is on shore. There is one boat.

2. The convention is in a wilderness retreat. No roads out. The crew arrived
   by bus. The bus leaves at 4pm.

3. HoneyCon is in a shopping mall. The honey vault is in the food court.
   The food court is full of children on a school trip.

4. The convention is in a hotel. The honey is on the penthouse floor. The
   bears have been assigned rooms in the basement.

5. HoneyCon is outdoors — a sprawling festival in a park. The honey is in a
   tent at the center. Every path to the tent passes through a different
   security checkpoint.
