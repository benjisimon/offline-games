# Lasers & Feelings

## Premise

You are the crew of the interstellar scout ship *Raptor*. Your mission: explore
uncharted space, deal with aliens, and defend the Consortium worlds.

Captain Darcy has been overcome by a strange psychic entity called Something
Else, and is recovering in a medical pod. You're on your own.

## Create Your Character

**1. Pick a style:** Alien / Android / Dangerous / Heroic / Hot-Shot / Intrepid / Savvy

**2. Pick a role:** Doctor / Envoy / Engineer / Explorer / Pilot / Scientist / Soldier

**3. Pick your number (2–5):**
* **High number (4–5):** better at LASERS — technology, science, cold rationality, calm precise action
* **Low number (2–3):** better at FEELINGS — intuition, diplomacy, seduction, wild passionate action

**4. Pick a character goal (or make up your own):**
Become Captain / Meet New Aliens / Shoot Bad Guys / Find New Worlds /
Solve Weird Space Mysteries / Prove Yourself / Keep Being Awesome

**5. Give your character a cool space name.** Like Sparks McGee or something.

**6. Pick your minifig.** That's you.

**7. Pick a sidekick.** Choose a buildable creature and describe one thing it's
good at — that's its power. Set it next to your minifig. When its power
applies to what you're doing, you get +1d on your roll.

**You have:** a Consortium uniform (with built-in vacc-suit), a
space-phone-camera-communicator-scanner (with universal translator), and a
variable-beam phase pistol (set to stun, usually).

## Create the Ship

As a group, pick **two strengths** for the Raptor:
Fast / Nimble / Well-Armed / Powerful Shields / Superior Sensors /
Cloaking Device / Fightercraft

Pick **one problem:**
Fuel Hog (always needs energy crystals) /
Only One Medical Pod (Captain Darcy is in it) /
Horrible Circuit Breakers (consoles explode in battle) /
Grim Reputation (Darcy did some bad stuff in the past)

## Your Number

Set a die in front of your minifig showing your number. It doesn't change
during the game — it's just there so everyone can see it.

## Rolling the Dice

When you do something risky, the GM tells you how many dice to roll:

* **Base:** 1d6
* **+1d** if you're prepared
* **+1d** if you're an expert

Roll all your dice and compare each one to your number.

**LASERS** (science, tech, reason) → you want to roll **under** your number.

**FEELINGS** (rapport, intuition, passion) → you want to roll **over** your number.

**Count your successes:**

* **0 successes** — it goes wrong. GM says how things get worse.
* **1 success** — you barely manage it. GM inflicts a complication, harm, or cost.
* **2 successes** — you do it well. Good job.
* **3 successes** — critical success! GM tells you some extra effect you get.

**LASER FEELINGS** — if any die in your roll lands on **exactly your number**
(even if other dice didn't succeed), you get a special insight. Ask the GM one question and they answer honestly:
* What are they really feeling?
* Who's behind this?
* How could I get them to ___?
* What should I be on the lookout for?
* What's really going on here?

(A LASER FEELINGS roll also counts as a success.)

## Helping

If you want to help someone else who's rolling, say how and make a roll.
If you succeed, give them +1d.

## GM Setup — Build the Threat

Roll or choose on each table:

**A threat...**
1. Zorgon the Conqueror / 2. The Hive Armada / 3. Rogue Captain
4. Space Pirates / 5. Cyber Zombies / 6. Alien Brain Worms

**Wants to...**
1. Destroy / Corrupt / 2. Steal / Capture / 3. Bond with
4. Protect / Empower / 5. Build / Synthesize / 6. Pacify / Occupy

**The...**
1. Space Pirate King/Queen / 2. Void Crystals / 3. Star Dreadnought
4. Quantum Tunnel / 5. Ancient Space Ruin / 6. Alien Artifact

**Which will...**
1. Destroy a solar system / 2. Reverse time / 3. Enslave a planet
4. Start a war / invasion / 5. Rip a hole in reality / 6. Fix Everything

## GM: Running the Game

Play to find out how they defeat the threat. Introduce it by showing evidence
of its recent badness. Before a threat does something to the characters, show
signs it's about to happen, then ask what they do.

Call for a roll when the situation is uncertain. Don't pre-plan outcomes — let
the chips fall where they may. Use failures to push the action forward.

Ask questions and build on the answers. *"Have any of you encountered a Void
Cultist before? Where? What happened?"*

## Supplies

* A bag of d6s — one per player (set to your number), plus rolling dice
* Story dice — roll for random complications or earned powers
* Lego minifigs for characters
* Buildable creatures for alien encounters or companions
* Foreign coins for rewards / mission tokens
