# Target Practice

## Goal

Build feel for the cue ball before playing a full game. Works as a warmup
or as a standalone game for beginners.

## Before You Start — Two Things That Matter

* **Bridge hand**: curl your index finger over the cue and rest your hand flat
  on the table. Don't hold the cue in the air.
* **Keep the cue level**: beginners tilt the back end up. A level cue goes
  where you aim.

Teach these two things once. Skip everything else.

---

## Game 1: Hand Roll

No cue required.

* Roll the cue ball with your hand toward a pocket
* Try to get it in — or as close as possible
* Take turns; closest to the pocket (or in it) wins the round
* Play to 5 rounds

Good for: zero technique, instant feedback, everyone laughs.

---

## Game 2: Rail Roulette

* Place the cue ball anywhere on the table
* Each player shoots at the far rail (the cushion at the opposite end)
* Goal: get the cue ball to stop as close to the rail as possible without touching it
* Touching or bouncing off = disqualified that round
* Closest wins the round; play to 5

Good for: teaching speed control with no pockets involved.

---

## Game 3: Corner Pocket Challenge

* Place 5 balls in a loose cluster near the center of the table
* Each player gets 5 shots; aim for one specific corner pocket
* Count how many you sink
* Highest count wins

Good for: practicing aim at a real target with low pressure.

---

## Conclusion

Once everyone has a feel for the table, move to Straight Pool or Cutthroat.
